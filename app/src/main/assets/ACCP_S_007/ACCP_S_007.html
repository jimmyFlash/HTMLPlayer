Module 2

  • • sthma is a disease with many variations (heterogeneous), usually characterized by chronic airway inflammation.
  • • Asthma has two key defining features:
    • - History of Respiratory Symptoms that vary over time and in intensity.
    • - Variable Expiratory Airflow limitation.
  • • The differential diagnosis in a patient with suspected asthma varies with age.
  • • Many patients (25–35%) with a diagnosis of asthma in primary care cannot be confirmed as having asthma.
  • • Schedule a routine review for asthma patients at least once a year.
  • • Asthma control means the extent to which the effects of asthma can be seen in the patient, or have been reduced or removed by treatment.
  • • Risk factors are factors that increase the patient’s future risk of having exacerbations (flare-ups), loss of lung function, or medication side-effects.
  • • Lung function should be recorded at diagnosis, 3–6 months after starting treatment, and periodically thereafter.
  • •The FEV1/FVC ratio is normally greater than 0.75 - 0.80, and usually > 0.90 in children.
