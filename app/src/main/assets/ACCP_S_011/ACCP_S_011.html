Module 6

  • • Asthma is a long-lasting disease which affects the airways characterized by recurrent attacks of wheezing, chest tightness, shortness of breath, and coughing. It affects people of all ages, but it most often starts during childhood.
  • • There are 2 types of asthma: allergic and non-allergic.
  • • Allergic asthmatic patients are younger and have a better response to conventional therapy particular to inhaled corticosteroids (ICS). Patients probably have a personal or family history of allergies, such as allergic rhinitis or hay fever, or eczema.
  • • Non-allergic asthmatic patients are often adult onset; it is associated with non-allergic co- morbidities, such as rhino-sinusitis and gastro-esophageal reflux, and is less responsive to inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) treatment.
  • • Nocturnal asthma: refers to asthma symptoms that seem worse in the middle of the night, typically between 2AM and 4AM.
  • • Occupational asthma: refers to asthma that is newly diagnosed and caused by exposure to a substance for example chemicals or animal proteins in the workplace.
  • • Allergic asthma triggers: dust mites, cockroach, pollen, molds and pets.
  • • Non allergic asthma triggers: smoke, exercise, cold air, chemical fumes, and pollution.
  • • Complications of asthma: Rarely can lead to pneumonia, collapse to a part or all the lung, respiratory failure, and Status asthmatics.
  • • When asthma is badly controlled lead to bad effect on quality of life, fatigue, underperformance or absence from work or school.
  • • Asthma medications: bronchodilators, anti-Inflammatories, combination Medicines, antibiotics and vaccinations.
  • • Bronchodilators either short acting or long acting; acts by relax the muscles around the airways.
  • • Anti-Inflammatories: reducing the swelling and mucus production inside the airways.
  • • Combination Medicines: as combining inhaled bronchodilators and inhaled corticosteroids.
  • • Antibiotics: in people with asthma who have flare-ups caused by bacterial or viral infections.
  • • Vaccinations: To protect against the flu one should be immunized every year .
  • • Along with medications, life style tips should be followed as; to avoid asthma triggers, stop smoking, regular exercise, take care in cold weather and eat healthy.
